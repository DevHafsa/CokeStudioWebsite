Coke Studio

About

Coke Studio is a Pakistani television show and international music franchise that features studio-recorded music performances by well-known and up-and-coming musicians. It is Pakistan's longest-running annual television music show and has aired since 2008.

From traditional classical, folk, Sufi, qawwali, ghazal, and bhangra music to current hip hop, rock, and pop music, Coke Studio incorporates diverse musical influences. The music shows Pakistan's multiculturalism by bringing together musicians from different parts of the country.

After musical performances were performed on a concert-like platform in Brazil in 2007, Nadeem Zaman of The Coca-Cola Company came up with the idea for the event. In 2008, former Vital Signs member Rohail Hyatt teamed up with Nadeem Zaman, then Marketing Head of The Coca-Cola Company, to establish a Pakistani version of the show. The first season premiered in front of a live audience on June 8, 2008. He along with his wife Umber Hyatt and Nofil Naqvi produced the show.

It became popular right away, winning praise from critics and routinely airing on many radio and television stations around the nation. Live audiences were excluded in season 2 and performances took place in a closed studio platform, a format that is still used today. Until season 6, Hyatt served as the show's executive producer before stepping out in 2013. Before Strings confirmed that season 10 would be their final production on October 29, 2017, he was replaced by members Bilal Maqsood and Faisal Kapadia. Season 11 will be produced by Ali Hamza and Zohaib Kazi, it was revealed on March 8th, 2018. After producing the season, they resigned. Later, Rohail Hyatt made a comeback for the show's twelfth season, which premiered on October 11, 2019. Hyatt kept things going through the 13th season, and on his advice, Xulfi took the helm for the 14th season with Abdullah Siddiqui serving as the associate producer, with Hyatt once more giving the lead.

In 2017, on the occasion of the show's tenth anniversary, Rizwan U. Khan, General Manager of Coca-Cola Pakistan & Afghanistan, said, "We have come a long way since we embarked on this challenging journey a decade ago. Looking back, we feel greatly humbled that Coke Studio has been able to achieve so much, in terms of bringing virtually unknown or little-known musicians into the national limelight, re-introducing music genres like qawwali and Sufi music to the youth of Pakistan, continuing to stay true to the promise of producing quality fusion of music and practice playing an important role in reviving the music industry of Pakistan."
